Examiner Instructions

  1. Read the stem aloud. Prompt the examinee to open the question stem file.
  2. Show clinical images when appropriate. Direct the examinee to open the corresponding image file at the right time during the case.
  3. Ask questions in order. Let the candidate drive; redirect only if they stall.
  4. Do not lead. Ask: "Are you sure?" or "Anything else?"
  5. Use evolutions. Pivot to evolution scenarios to test deeper knowledge.
  6. Note critical errors. Each case has specific automatic-fail errors.
  7. Grade independently. A candidate may pass one case and fail another.
  8. Provide written feedback. Specific comments help residents improve.
After each case, use the Score & Feedback tab to record your grade.

Scoring Guide — ABS Format

PASS

Safe, competent management

Correct diagnosis • Safe workup & treatment • Recognized decision points • Responded to evolutions • Minor gaps OK
MARGINAL

Significant knowledge gaps

Needed prompting • Partially correct with errors • Struggled with decisions • Multiple redirections • Concerning but not dangerous
FAIL

Unsafe decisions or critical deficiencies

Missed life-threat • Would harm patient • Can't describe operative steps • Critical error • Could not be redirected
