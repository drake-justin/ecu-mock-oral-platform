Examiner Instructions

  1. Read the stem aloud. Do not show the stem to the candidate unless instructed.
  2. Ask questions in order. Allow the candidate to drive the discussion; redirect only if they stall.
  3. Do not lead the candidate. If they are on the wrong path, ask: "Are you sure?" or "Is there anything else?"
  4. Use scenario evolutions. If time permits, pivot to the evolution scenarios to test deeper knowledge.
  5. Note critical errors immediately. Each case lists specific errors that warrant automatic failure.
  6. Grade each case independently. A candidate may pass one case and fail another in the same session.
  7. Provide written feedback. Brief, specific feedback helps the resident improve for the real boards.
Reminder: After each case, use the Score & Feedback tab to record your grade and comments.

Scoring Guide

ABS format. Each case receives one of three grades:

PASS

Safe, competent surgical management.

  • Correctly identified diagnosis or appropriate differential
  • Logical and safe workup and treatment plan
  • Recognized critical decision points
  • Responded appropriately to evolutions
  • Minor gaps OK if no unsafe decisions
MARGINAL

Significant knowledge gaps or inconsistencies.

  • Correct diagnosis but with significant prompting
  • Partially correct plan with errors or omissions
  • Struggled with decision-making or operative details
  • Required multiple redirections
  • No dangerous error, but raises concern
FAIL

Unsafe decisions or critical deficiencies.

  • Failed to recognize life-threatening condition
  • Management would result in patient harm
  • Unable to describe basic operative steps
  • Committed a critical error (see case list)
  • Could not be redirected to safe plan
